Case Study · 2024

FinTrack — Personal Finance Dashboard

Redesigning a fragmented fintech experience into a unified, intelligent financial command centre — resulting in a 40% spike in user conversion and a 65% jump in daily active usage.

RoleLead UI/UX Designer + Frontend Dev
Duration14 Weeks
TeamSolo + 2 Backend Engineers
PlatformWeb App (Desktop & Mobile)

01 · The Problem

FinTrack's existing product had users juggling 4–5 separate apps to track spending, savings, budgets, and investments. Churn was at 62% within the first 30 days. Exit interviews revealed a single consistent pain point: users felt overwhelmed, not empowered.

The challenge was not a lack of data — it was the lack of clarity, hierarchy, and meaning behind the data presented.

02 · Research & Discovery

📋 Methods Used

  • 12 in-depth user interviews
  • Competitor audit (Mint, YNAB, PiggyVest)
  • Heuristic evaluation of existing product
  • Card sorting & IA workshops
  • Analytics review (Mixpanel)

🔍 Key Findings

  • Users wanted a single "financial health score"
  • 78% checked their balance daily — on mobile
  • Goal tracking was the #1 requested feature
  • Alerts & notifications were ignored (poor timing)
  • Trust was built through transparency of data

03 · Design Process

→

I started by mapping a complete information architecture that reduced the primary navigation from 9 items to 4 core sections. Every screen was wireframed and tested with 8 participants before any visual design work began. A lean design system of 60+ components was built in Figma using auto-layout, ensuring the frontend build was pixel-precise and consistent.

04 · Key Design Decisions

Financial Health Score

A single glanceable score (0–100) computed from spending habits, savings rate, and goal progress — replaced 5 confusing graphs on the old home screen.

Smart Spending Rings

Category-based circular progress rings replaced bar charts. User testing showed 3× faster comprehension and higher perceived "simplicity."

Goal-First Onboarding

New users set 1 financial goal before seeing any data. This anchored their entire experience and reduced the "what do I do here?" confusion by 71%.

Contextual Notifications

Redesigned the alerts engine to trigger at psychologically optimal moments (e.g., payday, end of month) — open rates went from 11% to 49%.

Case Study · 2024

MediCore — Healthcare SaaS Platform

Designing a HIPAA-aligned, clinician-first patient management system that eliminated paper records across 12 clinics in Nigeria and Ghana — cutting administrative time by 70%.

RoleUI/UX Designer + Frontend Lead
Duration22 Weeks
Team2 Designers, 4 Engineers
PlatformWeb SaaS (Responsive)

01 · The Problem

Clinics across West Africa were managing patient records via physical folders, WhatsApp messages, and disconnected spreadsheets. Misplaced records led to repeated tests, delayed diagnoses, and billing errors worth millions of naira monthly.

MediCore needed a system that nurses, doctors, and administrators could actually use — not a complex enterprise tool that required two weeks of training.

02 · Research & Discovery

📋 Research Methods

  • Contextual inquiry at 3 clinics (Lagos, Abuja, Accra)
  • Role-based user interviews (Doctors, Nurses, Admin)
  • Shadow sessions with front desk staff
  • Competitive audit of Epic, Practo, Vezeeta
  • Accessibility audit for low-bandwidth environments

🔍 Critical Insights

  • Nurses are the primary system users, not doctors
  • Intermittent internet is a hard constraint
  • Trust in digital systems is built through speed, not features
  • Billing and scheduling were the highest-pain workflows
  • Role-based access control was a non-negotiable requirement

03 · Design Process

Field Research
→
Journey Mapping
→
Service Blueprint
→
Wireframes
→
Design System
→
Pilot Testing
→
Rollout

I created 3 distinct user journeys — doctor, nurse, and admin — each with tailored dashboards. A service blueprint revealed 17 handoff points between roles where delays occurred. The design prioritised resolving those specific handoffs. Every prototype was tested on-site at clinics with actual staff before progressing.

04 · Key Design Decisions

Offline-First Architecture

Designed the UI around an offline-first model using service workers. Patient lookups and record updates queue locally and sync when connectivity returns — critical for Nigerian network conditions.

Role-Based Dashboards

Three separate dashboard views with the same underlying data — each surfacing only what each role needs. Nurses see queues and vitals; doctors see clinical history; admins see billing and capacity.

One-Click Patient Check-In

Reduced patient registration from a 7-screen process to a 3-step flow using smart defaults, autofill from phone number lookup, and pre-populated visit templates.

Colour-Coded Triage System

A visual triage indicator system (green/amber/red) on the patient queue gave nurses at-a-glance severity awareness, eliminating verbal handoffs and missed priority cases.

Case Study · 2023

Bloom — Mobile E-Learning Platform

Transforming a 15% course completion rate into 85% through gamification, social accountability, and a mobile-first redesign built for African learners with limited data plans.

RoleLead Product Designer
Duration18 Weeks
TeamSolo Designer, 3 Engineers
PlatformiOS & Android (React Native)

01 · The Problem

Bloom's v1 app had 40,000 downloads but catastrophic engagement: only 15% of enrolled learners ever completed a course. Revenue was driven entirely by new sign-ups, not retention. The product was a content library pretending to be a learning platform.

The core problem: learning is hard, and the app was giving users every reason to quit. No community. No accountability. No sense of progress. No reason to return tomorrow.

02 · Research & Discovery

📋 Research Methods

  • Cohort dropout analysis (Mixpanel + Amplitude)
  • 15 learner interviews (completers vs dropouts)
  • Gamification framework audit (Octalysis)
  • Benchmark: Duolingo, Khan Academy, Coursera
  • Survey of 200 Bloom users on motivation triggers

🔍 Key Insights

  • 83% of dropouts quit in the first 3 days
  • Learners who joined a study group had 6× completion rates
  • Progress was invisible — users didn't know how far they'd come
  • Lessons were too long for mobile commutes (avg. 45 mins)
  • "I forgot about it" was the #1 dropout reason

03 · Design Process

Data Analysis
→
Interviews
→
Gamification Strategy
→
Mobile Wireframes
→
Prototype & Test
→

I applied the Octalysis gamification framework to identify the right motivational levers for adult learners. Unlike Duolingo's streak-heavy approach, Bloom's audience needed achievement-based motivation over loss-aversion. Lessons were restructured into 8–12 minute micro-modules. Every flow was A/B tested with cohorts of 500 users before full rollout.

04 · Key Design Decisions

Learning Streaks + XP System

A lightweight streaks mechanic paired with an XP (Experience Points) economy that unlocked badges, profile customisation, and certificate frames — giving learners visible social capital.

Study Circles

Cohort-based study groups of 5–8 learners with a shared leaderboard. Group progress was shown on each member's home screen, creating social accountability without pressure.

Micro-Module Architecture

Restructured all courses from 6 long lectures into 25–40 micro-modules of 8–12 minutes each. Users could complete a module during a commute — removing the "I don't have time" barrier.

Smart Reminders

AI-driven push notifications that learned each user's optimal engagement window based on their past activity — notifications sent at the right time, not just a fixed daily time.

Case Study · 2023

Verde — Sustainable Fashion Brand Website

Building an award-shortlisted, immersive brand experience for a Lagos-based eco-fashion startup — tripling organic traffic and converting 28% of visitors into email subscribers within 60 days of launch.

RoleUX Designer + Frontend Developer
Duration10 Weeks
TeamSolo
PlatformMarketing Website + CMS

01 · The Problem

Verde had a powerful story — sustainable, ethically made fashion designed in Lagos — but a website that looked like a basic Shopify template. Investors were unimpressed. Wholesale buyers weren't taking emails seriously. The brand's digital presence was destroying trust rather than building it.

The brief was clear: build a website worthy of the brand they had already built offline. The site needed to feel like walking into a high-end Lagos concept store, not clicking through a generic e-commerce template.

02 · Research & Discovery

📋 Research Methods

  • Brand immersion workshop (2-day)
  • Target audience interviews (10 eco-conscious consumers)
  • Benchmark study: Ganni, Veja, Patagonia, Cek
  • Awwwards & Siteinspire trend audit
  • Content audit and photography review

🔍 Key Insights

  • The brand story was the product — it needed to lead
  • Transparency (supply chain, materials) drove purchase intent
  • Scroll-driven storytelling outperformed static pages 3:1
  • Mobile was 74% of their existing traffic
  • Photography was premium — needed a canvas worthy of it

03 · Design Process

Brand Strategy
→
Moodboard
→
Site Architecture
→
Interaction Design
→
Hi-Fi Prototype
→
Next.js Build

CMS + Launch

I began with a 2-day brand immersion — studying Verde's fabrics, visiting their workshop, understanding their sourcing relationships. The visual language I developed was rooted in earth tones, organic textures, and generous white space. Every scroll animation was choreographed in Figma before a single line of code was written. The full site was built in Next.js with Framer Motion and integrated with Sanity CMS so the Verde team could update content independently.

04 · Key Design Decisions

Scroll-Driven Storytelling

The homepage is a linear narrative — from brand origin, to sourcing ethics, to craftsmanship, to the final product. Users scroll through a story, not a page. Average scroll depth reached 94%.

Material Transparency Module

An interactive section letting users explore each fabric — origin country, environmental impact, certifications. This transparency feature alone drove a 340% increase in time on site.

Full-Bleed Editorial Layout

Rejected conventional grid-based layouts in favour of full-bleed editorial compositions. Each product spread was treated as a magazine page — bold typography over immersive photography.

Performance Without Compromise

Despite rich animations and large images, the site achieves a Lighthouse performance score of 94. Every image uses next-gen formats, lazy loading, and responsive art direction via Next.js Image.
